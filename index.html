Job Market Paper

The Long Shadow: Childhood Poverty and the Returns to Education

with Agnieszka Postepska and Viola Angelini · GLO Discussion Paper No. 1731 (2026)

Abstract

This paper documents substantial heterogeneity in the returns to education by childhood poverty status among Indonesian wage workers aged 15–35. Using the IFLS panel and the Klein–Vella control-function estimator (identification via conditional heteroskedasticity, without exclusion restrictions), we find that individuals who grew up poor earn only 1.5% per additional year of schooling — less than one-fourth of the 6.8% earned by those never poor. Stronger positive selection among the poor, together with descriptive evidence of lower skill gains per year of schooling and restricted access to high-paying jobs, helps rationalize the gap, suggesting that expanding access to schooling alone may be insufficient to promote equality.

Working Papers

The Cold Shock: Poverty Impacts of Extreme Winter in Mongolia

with Lydia Kim · Background paper, World Bank 2026 Mongolia Poverty Assessment (work in progress)

Abstract

We provide the first household-panel estimate of the poverty cost of a cold-extreme shock. A difference-in-differences design on a nationally representative HSES panel exploits spatial variation in the severity of Mongolia’s 2023/24 dzud, a severe winter that killed over eight million livestock. In the worst-hit areas the dzud prevented a 15.2 percentage-point reduction in poverty during a major national decline; a triple difference locates the effect almost entirely among herders, whose poverty rose from 20% to 32%. Asset destruction is the principal channel — livestock income accounts for roughly 71% of herder income loss — with suggestive evidence that livestock insurance buffers the shock.
